// 01 — Foundations

Program Philosophy

01
Speed Is Built Aerobically

80% of your running will be easy — truly easy. Zone 2 aerobic base work teaches your body to burn fat efficiently and supports higher training volume without accumulated fatigue. Speed comes from a deep aerobic engine, not just hard intervals.

02
Joints Before Load

Knees Over Toes and Strength Side protocols build bulletproof ankles, knees, and hips before we add heavy barbell work. The goal is tissue capacity first — this eliminates the #1 runner injury pattern and unlocks Olympic lifting safely.

03
Technique Precedes Intensity

Squat cleans and snatches are skill movements. Weeks 1–3 are exclusively technique — PVC pipe and empty bar only. Load increases only after movement patterns are automated. Rushing this phase is the single biggest mistake beginner Olympic lifters make.

04
Progression Is Planned, Not Random

The 8 weeks are divided into three clear gears: Foundation (wks 1–3), Build (wks 4–6), and Sharpen + Taper (wks 7–8). Each block has a defined purpose. Week 8 is your taper and race week — your body will be primed. Trust the structure.

// 04 — Olympic Lifting Progression

Squat Clean & Snatch

You haven't touched these lifts in years — treat yourself as a beginner regardless of your general fitness. The first 4 weeks are purely technical. PVC pipe and empty barbell only. Speed of progression depends on movement quality, not strength.

Weeks 1–3: Technique Only
PVC pipe → empty bar (45 lbs). Movement pattern drills only. No load until week 3.
  • Clean Pull / Romanian Deadlift Drill
    Learn hip hinge, bar path close to body, big jump extension
    4 × 6
  • Hang Power Clean
    Start above knee. Triple extension → fast elbows → front rack. PVC first 2 wks, bar week 3.
    5 × 4
  • Front Squat Hold
    Rack position practice. Elbows up, chest tall. Pause 2 sec at bottom.
    4 × 5
  • Snatch Grip Overhead Squat
    PVC only. Active shoulder press into bar overhead. Deepest mobility work you'll do.
    4 × 5
  • Power Snatch (Hang)
    Wide grip, bar stays close. Jump, shrug, pull under fast. PVC + empty bar.
    5 × 3
Weeks 4–6: Loading Phase
Begin adding weight. Load only when reps feel automatic. Increase 5–10 lbs max per week.
  • Squat Clean (Full)
    Floor to full front squat catch. Start at 50–55% of estimated max. Focus on catching deep.
    5 × 3
  • Hang Squat Clean
    Reinforce the pull and catch. Keep loads modest — speed > weight here.
    4 × 3
  • Power Snatch → Overhead Squat complex
    Catch in power position, then descend into overhead squat. Builds confidence in the catch.
    4 × 2+2
  • Full Squat Snatch
    Introduce week 6 only after power snatch + OHS feels solid. Light weight, perfect reps only.
    4 × 2
  • Front Squat (moderate)
    Builds catch strength for the clean. Not a max effort — accessory movement.
    3 × 5
Weeks 7–8: Sharpen & Taper
Reduce lifting volume in week 7. Week 8 is maintenance only — protect legs for race day.
  • Squat Clean — Heavy Singles / Doubles
    Work up to challenging but not max effort. 80–85% intensity. Chase technique at heavier loads.
    5 × 2
  • Squat Snatch
    Main snatch variation. Full squat catch. Build confidence at moderate weights.
    5 × 2
  • Clean & Jerk (intro)
    Optional: add the jerk in weeks 9–10. Push press → push jerk progression.
    3 × 2
  • Week 8: Maintenance Only
    2 sessions max. Light technique work only. No new loads. Preserve race-day legs.
    Low vol
Accessory Work (Every Session)
Supports the Olympic lifts and protects your body from running volume.
  • Romanian Deadlift
    Hip hinge strength. Hamstring + posterior chain. 3 sec eccentric.
    3 × 8
  • Single-Leg Glute Bridge
    Runner hip stability. Controls knee valgus under load.
    3 × 12
  • Pallof Press
    Anti-rotation core work. Critical for Olympic lifting and running economy.
    3 × 12
  • DB Rows / Pull-Ups
    Upper back strength for the clean front rack and overhead position.
    3 × 8–10

// 05 — Mobility & Joint Work

KOT + Strength Side Protocol

These protocols run 3–4× per week — they're your pre-lift warm-up and running recovery system. Search YouTube for each creator for video demonstrations. Time investment: 15–20 minutes per session.

Knees Over Toes Guy — ATG Training
Knee & Lower Body Restoration
  • ATG Split Squat — The cornerstone. Knee tracking over pinky toe, full range of motion. Start bodyweight, add load over weeks. Rebuilds patellar tendon and quad strength through full range.
  • Patrick Step / Tibialis Raise — Shin strength and ankle dorsiflexion. Do 2–3 × 25 daily. Prevents shin splints and foot strike issues in runners.
  • Reverse Sled Walk / Nordic Curl — If you have access to a sled, reverse drags are gold. Nordics protect hamstrings from running eccentric load.
  • ATG Calf Raise (single leg) — Slow, full range (heel below foot level). Bulletproofs the Achilles. 3 × 15 each leg.
  • Hip Flexor Stretch + Couch Stretch — Reverses sitting tightness. Hold 2 min each side. Critical for clean squat depth.
Strength Side — Athletic Mobility
Hip, Ankle & Overhead Mobility
  • 90/90 Hip Rotation Flow — Internal and external hip rotation. Daily habit. Directly transfers to squat clean depth and running stride freedom.
  • Ankle Dorsiflexion Progression — Wall ankle stretches + banded distraction. Non-negotiable for overhead squat in snatch. Do every session.
  • Thoracic Spine Opener — T-spine rotation and extension on foam roller. Opens the overhead position for snatch. 10 reps per side.
  • Deep Squat Hold Progression — Assisted deep squat with door frame or rack. Work toward 5-min hold over 12 weeks. Transforms clean catch position.
  • Shoulder Capsule Work — Banded shoulder distraction + sleeper stretch. Protects overhead position in snatch, prevents rotator cuff irritation from volume.
Running-Specific Recovery
Post-Run Routine (10 min)
  • Standing Hip Flexor Stretch — 90 sec each side. Addresses stride restriction from heavy run volume.
  • Pigeon Pose — 2 min each side after long runs. Glute and external rotator recovery.
  • Calf + Achilles Downward Dog Walk — Alternating heel presses. 60 sec. Flushes the lower leg post-run.
  • Supine Figure-4 — Piriformis and IT band adjacent. 90 sec each side. Add on interval days.
  • Diaphragmatic Breathing — 5 min of slow belly breathing post-run. Activates parasympathetic recovery. Speeds adaptation.
Before Every Lifting Session
10-Min Barbell Prep Warm-Up
  • World's Greatest Stretch — 5 reps each side. Hip flexor, thoracic, and hamstring all at once. Best pre-lift mobility drill.
  • PVC Overhead Squat — 3 × 5. Diagnoses your tightest links — ankles, hips, or thoracic. Work on what's tight.
  • Banded Ankle Distraction — 15 reps each side, in squat position. Immediately increases dorsiflexion for clean depth.
  • Hip 90/90 + Shoulder CARs — Controlled articular rotations. Prepares joints through full active range before loading.
  • Snatch Grip Press (PVC) — 2 × 10 overhead press with wide grip. Activates rotator cuff and scapular stabilizers before snatch work.

// 06 — The Arc

8-Week Progression Map

Weeks 1–3 — Phase 1: Foundation
Build The Engine

Running volume is low and easy — this is intentional. Your tendons, joints, and aerobic system are adapting. Lifting is technique-only with PVC and empty bar through week 2, then introduces the empty barbell in week 3. Mobility is introduced as a daily habit. Don't be tempted to run faster or add weight — the body is quietly building.

Weeks 4–6 — Phase 2: Build
Add Intensity & Load

Now you earn the hard work. 800m intervals, mile repeats, and tempo runs introduce real speed stress. Long runs push to 7–8 miles. Olympic lifting adds load for the first time — start at 50–55% of estimated max and add 5–10 lbs per session only when form is clean. Your mobility baseline is set, so joint prep becomes more targeted.

Week 7 — Phase 3: Sharpen
Race-Specific Work

The key workout of the entire program: 4 continuous miles at 10K race pace. This teaches your body and mind to hold the effort. Long run is cut to 7 miles to begin protecting your legs. Lifting volume drops — two sessions, moderate loads, technique focus only. This week should feel hard but not wrecked.

Week 8 — Taper & Race
Execute

Volume drops sharply. Short easy runs with crisp strides keep your neuromuscular system primed without accumulating fatigue. Lifting is one light session maximum. Sleep 8+ hours every night. Trust that the work is done — adaptation happens during recovery, not during the final week. Race day: go out at exactly 7:00/mi. Don't let the crowd pull you out too fast.

// 07 — Key Rules

Non-Negotiables

Never Skip Recovery Weeks

There are no dedicated recovery weeks in this 8-week plan — the taper IS your recovery. This means you must not add extra volume on feel-good days during the program. If you feel great on an easy day, that means the training is working. Stick to the script.

Lifting & Running Fatigue Stack

Olympic lifting taxes the same energy system as interval running. Never do a max effort lift the day before a speed workout. Tuesday/Thursday lifting, Monday/Saturday hard running creates the necessary separation.

Easy Runs Must Be Easy

If you can't maintain a conversation on an easy day, you're too fast. Use a HR monitor: stay under 75% of max HR. Most runners run easy days 30–45 sec/mile too fast and wonder why they're always tired.

Technique Failures = Stop Lifting

In the snatch and clean, if the bar crashes, you round your back, or you miss a catch, the weight is too heavy. Strip 10–15 lbs and rebuild from there. Form breaks are always a sign of too much load, not insufficient effort.
